About

Hi there! I am a senior in the Engineering & Design Academy at Dublin High School. Leading and engaging in hands-on engineering projects at my academy has paved my way to learn and explore a variety of concepts in science and technology. I see myself as a creative person. Unlike many professions, coding has its own originality, where you create programs from scratch, using unique algorithms. I feel empowered when I make something come to life on my computer. The feeling I get when I successfully code a program is indescribable. The long hours working on the program is finally worth the time spent. It gives me a sense of accomplishment. My love for programming and coding first started at the age of ten, when I received a robotics set for Christmas. I built the robot and controlled the features and functionalities of it, by using an online programming application. I want to continue my education in the field of computer science. I envision myself graduating from a college with degrees in computer science and business. I aspire to do the M.E.T. (Management, Entrepreneurship, Technology) program in the University of Berkeley.

The Engineering and Design Academy has helped me nurture my knowledge in computer science and given me the confidence to explore these skills further down the career line. The academy gave me an opportunity to attend Python coding workshops at University of California Berkeley that roused my eagerness to explore more coding languages, including HTML, CSS, and Java, in the future.

Apart from coding and programming, my life revolves around Bharatanatyam (an Indian classical dance). I have been professionally dancing since the age of six and have had the privilege to perform at many public venues, including on NBC TV. This kind of demanding environment brings the discipline, determination, perseverance, and creativity in me.
